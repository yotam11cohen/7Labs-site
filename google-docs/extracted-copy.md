## Study Design (from IRB)

**Study title:**
"Comprehensive Hairomic Analysis of Biomarkers in Individuals Exposed to Terror Attack Trauma"
Protocol ID: HAIROMIC-7 | Version 1.0, July 2024
Site: Soroka University Medical Center, Be'er-Sheva, Israel

**One-sentence study design (patient-friendly):**
This is an observational pilot study enrolling 30 participants who were directly exposed to the October 7th, 2023 terrorist attack; a single hair sample (at least 18 cm) is collected from each participant and analyzed using LC-MS/MS to measure changes in stress hormones, sex hormones, and endocannabinoids before and after the traumatic event.

**Study rationale (from IRB background section):**

> "Given the significant psychological toll of terror attacks, such as those on October 7th, 2023, it is crucial to explore innovative methods for assessing the physiological and endocrinological impacts of trauma. Our study aims to leverage hairomic analysis technology to gain insights into these impacts by using Liquid Chromatography-Tandem Mass Spectrometry (LC-MS/MS) to analyze sex hormones, stress hormones, and endocannabinoids."

**Primary objective:**
To evaluate the physiological and endocrinological changes in individuals exposed to terror attacks through hairomic analysis.

**Study significance (from IRB):**

> "By identifying biomarkers associated with trauma recovery and resilience, the study may lead to better screening, diagnosis, and treatment of PTSD and other stress-related disorders. Furthermore, the findings could pave the way for future research and development of personalized treatment strategies, contributing to improved mental health outcomes and quality of life for individuals affected by trauma."

## Scientific Claims

### What hair metabolomics can detect / reveal

1. **Long-term stress record:** Hair cortisol concentration (HCC) reflects integrated cortisol levels over months to years — not just a snapshot like blood or saliva. Each 1 cm of hair represents approximately one month of physiological history.

2. **Over 10,000 endogenous metabolites:** A study by Tao Huan et al. (University of British Columbia) identified over 10,000 endogenous metabolites in human hair, demonstrating the vast potential of hair analysis for metabolic profiling.

3. **Biomarkers captured in hair (partial list from grant + IRB):**

   | Category | Biomarker |
   |---|---|
   | Steroid Hormone | Cortisol |
   | Steroid Hormone | Cortisone |
   | Steroid Hormone | Androstenedione |
   | Steroid Hormone | Progesterone |
   | Steroid Hormone | Testosterone |
   | Endocannabinoid | 2-Arachidonoylglycerol (2-AG) |
   | Endocannabinoid | Anandamide (AEA) |
   | Endocannabinoid | Oleoylethanolamide (OEA) |
   | Endocannabinoid | Palmitoylethanolamide (PEA) |

4. **PTSD-linked biomarkers:** A study by Wilker et al. (2016) showed endocannabinoid concentrations in hair are associated with PTSD symptom severity.

5. **Sex hormones correlate with serum levels:** A study by Wen Han et al. (1998) demonstrated that hair testosterone levels were positively correlated with serum testosterone levels, suggesting hair analysis can be a reliable indicator of circulating sex hormone levels.

6. **Retrospective monthly tracking:** By segmenting hair into 1 cm sections, the analysis enables retrospective monthly tracking of biomarker levels — allowing reconstruction of physiological state before and after a traumatic event.

### Key product claims (from grant application)

- **"Stressomic analysis"** — stress-specific metabolomic profiling of individual health state with retrospective dynamics and prospective extrapolation.
- Goes beyond single-hormone (cortisol-only) testing to a **comprehensive multi-biomarker panel** using LC-MS/MS.
- Uses **proprietary machine learning algorithms** to translate complex spectral data into actionable clinical insights.
- Intended use cases include: chronic stress monitoring, PTSD screening and monitoring, pre/post-trauma assessment, personalized treatment guidance, population-level screening, and clinical research/drug development.

### Functional innovation claims (from grant application)

> "Minimally Invasive Sampling: Hair collection offers a patient-friendly alternative to traditional biofluid sampling."

> "Retrospective Analysis: Archivable nature of hair enables investigation of metabolic status long after the sample collection date."

> "Temporal Biomonitoring: Provides longitudinal metabolic information, offering distinct advantages over single-point blood/urine analyses."

## Ethical Framework / Participant Protections (from IRB)

- Full written informed consent required from each participant before any data collection.
- Ethics Committee (EC) and hospital General Director approval required before study commencement.
- Unique subject identification codes used to maintain confidentiality while enabling data traceability.
- Participants may withdraw at any time; reason is recorded but participation is voluntary.
- Adverse events are monitored and reported to the EC within 48 hours of any serious event.
- Study site: Soroka University Medical Center — a 1,000-bed university-affiliated referral center serving over 700,000 people in Southern Israel.
- The only anticipated adverse event is minor scissor cuts to the scalp during hair sample collection.
- All data obtained prospectively; data retained by the clinical research center with appropriate source documentation.
